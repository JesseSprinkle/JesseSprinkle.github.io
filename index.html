About Me

I was born and raised in a small town on the Texas coast named Rockport. My five sisters and I grew up climbing trees and rewatching the same VHS movies over and over, which is something I miss. Our household was musical, my father and grandfather being part time musicians, so at age eight I picked up the piano. While I regrettably quit after three years, I still on occasion sit down and play a piece. I hope to eventually relearn the instrument because it's one thing I enjoy very much.

I always had an aptitude for computers, starting my programming escapades making simple Roblox games and messing around in Scratch. While I didn't have my own computer until I was 16, I used the family machine for all my "fun" projects (which thankfully have been lost to time). I loved programming, but I didn't have the guidance to progress my skills until my junior year of high school. I was lucky enough to have one teacher who taught and led me on the path to becoming a stand out computer scientist, placing 5th at the state-wide competition my senior year.

Aside from programming, I have multiple other hobbies. Video games were something I loved as a teenager, and I still enjoy them on occasion, though more to socialize with my friends than for the games themselves. In an attempt to not stare at a screen for work, school, and entertainment, I spend a lot of my time doing other activities like reading books. Most recently, I've been reading Ernest Hemingway who is an excellent author I highly recommend. I also enjoy walks in the park, listening to music (especially classical), and simply socializing with my friends in person.

Post high school, I decided to attend Texas A&M University to get a degree in Computer Science and, consequently, a minor in Mathematics. I won't pretend I am the hardest working student, but I absolutely love learning and believe you die the day you lose your willingness to learn. There are many options for post-college careers, so I hope to find something that will fulfill my need to constantly be learning.
